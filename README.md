## Core Ideas

### Continuity Intelligence

AI should not only generate responses. In sensitive contexts, it should preserve context, provenance, and meaning without drifting from the source record.

### 51/49 Constraint

The human origin retains majority authority. AI may render, preserve, classify, and assist, but it cannot become the owner or final interpreter of the identity it carries.

### Renderer, Not Interpreter

AI should surface and organize verified records. It should not autonomously declare what a human life, memory, or grief means.

### Compression Is Identity

Human identity is not infinite data. It is a compressed continuity signal made of memory, values, relationships, emotional salience, voice, judgment, humor, and constraint.

### Provenance Before Fluency

A fluent answer without a source boundary is not trustworthy. The future of AI depends on knowing what is verified, what is inferred, what is generated, and what should remain unresolved.
