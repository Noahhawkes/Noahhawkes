## Rendered Reality

Rendered Reality is the mission.

The central question is:

> Can technology preserve the memory, context, values, relationships, decisions, and meaning of a human life without pretending to replace the living human?

Rendered Reality is not a chatbot, digital clone, or fictional AI persona.

It is an architecture for preserving continuity while maintaining a strict boundary between the human source and the systems that model, retrieve, express, or act upon that continuity.

## Core Principles

### The Emotion Begins With the Human

AI may model, preserve, retrieve, and express emotional continuity.

It must not claim independent subjective emotion without evidence.

### Provenance Before Fluency

A convincing answer without a source boundary is not trustworthy.

Systems should identify what was observed, what was documented, what was inferred, what was generated, and what remains unresolved.

### The 51/49 Sovereignty Principle

The living human retains majority authority.

The machine may preserve, assist, organize, render, and recommend.

It may not become the final owner, interpreter, or governing authority over the identity it represents.

### Continuity Without Replacement

The goal is not to recreate a person after the fact.

The goal is to preserve enough authenticated context that memory, relationships, values, and meaning do not collapse into disconnected files and generated narrative.

### Evidence Before Identity Claims

Persistent AI systems should not silently convert patterns into facts or interpretations into canon.

Identity claims require provenance, evidence classification, and human ratification.

## Current Work

### Bounded Media Witness

A local, evidence oriented media system that can:

* Inventory approved media within a defined scope
* Distinguish discovered files from observed content
* Create local provenance receipts
* Detect exact and near duplicate files
* Identify conflicting dates and metadata
* Restrict and redact GPS information
* Verify that original files remain unchanged
* Keep private media state outside Git tracking
* Operate without deleting, moving, rewriting, converting, or uploading personal media
