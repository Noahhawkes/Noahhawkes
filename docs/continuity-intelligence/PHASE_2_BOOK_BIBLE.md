# 3. Final Premise

Modern AI is fluent but amnesiac. It predicts well and often remembers nothing that matters.

That is not merely a memory problem. It becomes an identity problem and then a sovereignty problem.

A system that cannot carry a person's verified history into the present may fill the gap with invention. Invention presented as memory becomes a quiet form of theft.

This book follows a non-traditional builder who began by trying to preserve everything, then discovered that preserving a person honestly required separating five things that most systems blur together:

1. observation
2. inference
3. authority
4. action
5. memory

The mission did not disappear when the boundaries arrived. The boundaries made the mission possible.

# 7. Evidence Rules

The book must practice the architecture it describes.

Every implementation claim must use an accurate status:

- designed
- specified
- prototyped
- reported
- tested per document
- independently tested
- verified
- operational

These terms are not interchangeable.

Standing distinction:

> The document says implemented.  
> The document reports tests passed.  
> Runtime verification remains open.  
> Current operational truth remains UNKNOWN without a live receipt.

External AI summaries and repeated claims do not become true through repetition.

Excluded unsupported claims include, but are not limited to:

- one-million-times compression efficiency
- unsupported identity-consistency percentages
- fabricated enterprise statistics
- unearned academic or professional titles
- consciousness transfer presented as fact
- quantum or multiversal claims presented as completed engineering

The exclusion process is itself part of the story because the SOV1 synthesis performed this correction internally.
