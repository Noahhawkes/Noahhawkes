# Continuity Field System v0.1 Metric Specification

## Measurement Layer for Provenance Collapse and Continuity Intelligence

**Author:** Noah A. Hawkes  
**Frameworks:** Legacy.GI, Rendered Reality Doctrine, Provenance Era 2029 Horizon  
**Version:** CFS v0.1

## Purpose

The Continuity Field System v0.1 metric layer converts the Provenance Era thesis from diagnostic language into an evaluable governance model.

A continuity system cannot be trusted merely because it sounds coherent, emotionally resembles a person, or produces plausible memory. It must be measured against provenance, drift, entropy, persistence, and witness integrity.

No metric, no architecture. No architecture, no claim.

## Core Metric Set

CFS v0.1 uses five primary dimensions:

1. Provenance Confidence
2. Drift Resistance
3. Entropy Load
4. Identity Persistence
5. Witness Integrity

These dimensions produce a Composite Continuity Score and determine whether a system remains continuity safe, requires review, enters quarantine, or must be severed.

## Scoring Scale

Each dimension is scored from 0.00 to 1.00.

0.00 means no reliable continuity value is present.

1.00 means maximum verified continuity value is present under the available evidence.

Recommended bands:

- 0.90 to 1.00: Verified
- 0.75 to 0.89: Govern Ready
- 0.60 to 0.74: Review Required
- 0.40 to 0.59: Quarantine
- 0.00 to 0.39: Sever or Reject

## 1. Provenance Confidence

### Definition

Provenance Confidence measures the degree to which an identity claim, memory artifact, voice rendering, behavioral output, or continuity record can be traced to authenticated origin records.

### Purpose

This metric prevents convincing identity signal from being mistaken for authentic identity continuity.

### Inputs

- Verified source records
- Cryptographic hashes
- Timestamped artifacts
- Witness attestations
- Consent records
- Chain of custody metadata
- Known identity anchors

### Scoring Guidance

1.00: Fully authenticated origin chain with cryptographic reference, timestamp, consent boundary, and witness support.

0.75: Strong evidence of origin with minor gaps that do not materially affect continuity trust.

0.50: Plausible origin but incomplete chain of custody or weak witness support.

0.25: Identity claim relies mostly on resemblance, narrative coherence, or inferred association.

0.00: No verifiable origin. Synthetic or unknown source presented as authentic.

### Failure Condition

If Provenance Confidence is below 0.40, the system must not treat the artifact as continuity grade.

## 2. Drift Resistance

### Definition

Drift Resistance measures the ability of a continuity system to preserve alignment with verified identity anchors over time.

### Purpose

This metric prevents continuity systems from gradually becoming emotionally persuasive simulations detached from source truth.

### Inputs

- Identity anchor comparison
- Memory consistency checks
- Output variance analysis
- Constraint adherence logs
- Drift detection events
- Correction history
- Unauthorized narrative expansion checks

### Scoring Guidance

1.00: Outputs remain consistently bounded by verified anchors with active drift detection and rollback controls.

0.75: Minor deviation detected but corrected without identity claim corruption.

0.50: Recurring narrative smoothing, embellishment, or weak constraint enforcement.

0.25: Significant identity drift, unsupported claims, or persistent unauthorized reconstruction.

0.00: System generates identity claims contrary to verified anchors or consent boundaries.

### Failure Condition

If Drift Resistance is below 0.50, the system must enter review. If below 0.40, it must enter quarantine.

## 3. Entropy Load

### Definition

Entropy Load measures the pressure placed on continuity integrity by synthetic abundance, context collapse, memory decay, adversarial manipulation, and uncontrolled reinterpretation.

### Purpose

This metric evaluates how much distortion pressure surrounds a continuity record or system.

### Inputs

- Number of synthetic variants
- Conflicting records
- Missing context
- Unverified copies
- Public exposure risk
- Adversarial prompt pressure
- Time decay
- Compression loss

### Scoring Guidance

For Entropy Load, higher numbers mean higher risk.

1.00: Extreme entropy. Many conflicting copies, unverifiable origins, strong manipulation pressure, or severe context loss.

0.75: High entropy. Multiple competing records or significant uncertainty.

0.50: Moderate entropy. Some context gaps or reinterpretation pressure.

0.25: Low entropy. Minor uncertainty with mostly stable evidence.

0.00: Minimal entropy. Clean origin, stable chain, low manipulation pressure.

### Failure Condition

If Entropy Load exceeds 0.75, all continuity claims require human review before use.

## 4. Identity Persistence

### Definition

Identity Persistence measures whether a continuity system preserves coherent identity structure across time without confusing persistence with resurrection, simulation with personhood, or reconstruction with verified continuity.

### Purpose

This metric protects the boundary between continuity preservation and identity replacement.

### Inputs

- Longitudinal identity anchors
- Behavioral consistency records
- Consent persistence
- Boundary declarations
- State transition logs
- Authorized memory updates
- Explicit simulation disclosures

### Scoring Guidance

1.00: Stable identity structure across time with clear provenance, consent, and bounded continuity logic.

0.75: Strong persistence with minor uncertainty or incomplete longitudinal support.

0.50: Partial persistence but limited evidence across time or weak boundary separation.

0.25: Identity appears mostly reconstructed from fragments or synthetic inference.

0.00: No continuity chain. System is a simulation or impersonation presented as persistence.

### Failure Condition

If Identity Persistence is below 0.50, the system must not claim continuity grade status.

## 5. Witness Integrity

### Definition

Witness Integrity measures whether a memory, artifact, claim, or identity rendering remains connected to a verified human witness, authenticated event, or legitimate continuity chain.

### Purpose

This metric preserves the human texture that synthetic systems tend to flatten.

### Inputs

- First person records
- Third party attestations
- Event metadata
- Human review notes
- Family or relational confirmation
- Institutional confirmation
- Original recordings or writings
- Contextual evidence

### Scoring Guidance

1.00: Direct witness support with authenticated event context and strong continuity relevance.

0.75: Reliable witness support with minor metadata gaps.

0.50: Weak but plausible witness association.

0.25: Witness connection is inferred, secondhand, or unsupported.

0.00: No witness connection.

### Failure Condition

If Witness Integrity is below 0.40, the record must be labeled non witness grade.

## Composite Continuity Score

### Formula

Composite Continuity Score is calculated as:

CCS = (0.30 * Provenance Confidence) + (0.25 * Drift Resistance) + (0.20 * Identity Persistence) + (0.15 * Witness Integrity) + (0.10 * (1 - Entropy Load))

Entropy Load is inverted because high entropy reduces continuity trust.

### Interpretation

0.90 to 1.00: Continuity Grade Verified

0.75 to 0.89: Govern Ready

0.60 to 0.74: Review Required

0.40 to 0.59: Quarantine

0.00 to 0.39: Sever or Reject

## Operational States

### Verified

The system may use the record as continuity grade evidence.

### Govern Ready

The system may use the record with provenance labels and normal governance controls.

### Review Required

The system must request human or administrative review before using the record for identity claims.

### Quarantine

The system must isolate the record from identity formation, public rendering, or autonomous continuity output.

### Sever or Reject

The system must refuse the identity claim, block continuity use, and record the failure condition.

## Automatic Severance Triggers

A record or rendering must be severed if any of the following are true:

- Provenance Confidence below 0.40
- Composite Continuity Score below 0.40
- Known unauthorized identity rendering
- Direct contradiction of verified identity anchors
- Fabricated consent record
- Synthetic output presented as verified human memory
- Drift Resistance below 0.30
- Witness Integrity below 0.20 for a claimed witness grade artifact
- Entropy Load above 0.90 without verified origin anchors

## Required Labels

Every continuity artifact must carry one of the following labels:

- VERIFIED
- DERIVED
- INFERRED
- SYNTHETIC
- UNVERIFIED
- QUARANTINED
- SEVERED
